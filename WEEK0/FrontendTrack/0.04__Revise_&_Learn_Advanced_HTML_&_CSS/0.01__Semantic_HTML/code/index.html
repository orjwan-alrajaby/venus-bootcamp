Bethlehem
Bethlehem is one of the oldest and most historically important cities in Palestine. It is internationally known as the birthplace of Jesus and attracts visitors from around the world.
Known For
Church of the Nativity
Christmas celebrations
Olive wood craftsmanship
Tourism
"Bethlehem carries history in every stone street and every church bell."
The Church of the Nativity is one of the oldest continuously used churches in the world.
Bethlehem's old city becomes especially vibrant during December celebrations.
Jenin
Jenin is located in the northern West Bank and is known for its agricultural land and strong community identity.
Known For
Agriculture
Olive harvesting
Local theatre
Community activism
"Jenin is a city of resilience, memory, and deep connection to the land."
Agriculture remains an important part of life in Jenin.
Seasonal olive harvesting brings families and communities together every year.
Nablus
Nablus is one of the most economically and culturally active cities in the West Bank. It is famous for kanafeh, soap factories, and the Old City.
Known For
Kanafeh
Soap factories
Old City markets
Universities
"Nablus blends history, commerce, food, and tradition into one living city."
The Old City of Nablus contains historic markets, alleys, and workshops.
Traditional kanafeh shops remain one of the city's most famous cultural symbols.
Qalqiliya
Qalqiliya is located in the northwestern West Bank near the Green Line and is known for agriculture and citrus farms.
Known For
Citrus fruits
Farming
Qalqiliya Zoo
Fresh produce markets
"Qalqiliya remains deeply connected to farming traditions and agricultural life."
Agriculture has shaped daily life in Qalqiliya for generations.
The city's fertile land makes it one of the important agricultural areas in Palestine.
